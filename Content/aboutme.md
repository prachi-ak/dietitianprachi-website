About Me:
I am Prachi, an HCPC-registered dietitian with over 14 years of experience in clinical
nutrition, metabolic health, and integrative lifestyle medicine approaches.
My work combines evidence-based nutrition with a personalised, root-cause-informed
approach to support long-term health outcomes. I specialise in metabolic health, gut health,
PCOS and women’s health, weight management, autoimmune conditions, oncology (cancer)
nutrition, and chronic disease management, including diabetes, hypertension,
cardiovascular health, thyroid dysfunction, and insulin resistance.
Over the years, I have worked across hospital, clinical, and private healthcare settings,
supporting individuals with complex and long-term health conditions through personalised,
evidence-based nutrition and lifestyle interventions tailored to their medical needs, lifestyle,
and cultural preferences.
In a previous senior clinical role within an integrative healthcare setting, I managed a global
caseload across the UK, US, Canada, Europe, UAE, Singapore and India through remote
consultations and long-term lifestyle coaching. Alongside supporting individuals with
metabolic and chronic health conditions, I also led the oncology (cancer care) nutrition and
lifestyle vertical, working with individuals through treatment, recovery, and long-term
supportive care as part of a multidisciplinary approach.
My approach goes beyond simply prescribing meal plans. I focus on helping individuals build
sustainable habits around nutrition, movement, sleep, stress management, and overall
wellbeing - because long-term health outcomes are rarely driven by food alone.
Having worked extensively with South Asian and international populations, I understand the
importance of culturally relevant, realistic nutrition and lifestyle guidance that fits into
everyday life.
My aim is to help individuals feel informed, supported, and empowered to make sustainable
changes that improve both their health and quality of life.

My Approach:
I believe your lifestyle forms the blueprint of your health, and when understood and
optimised, it becomes the foundation for sustainable wellbeing.
My approach is rooted in evidence-based dietetics, combined with a personalised and
practical understanding of each individual’s health, lifestyle, and medical needs. I focus on
identifying and addressing the underlying factors driving health conditions, rather than
solely managing symptoms. Nutrition and lifestyle are powerful, yet often underutilised
tools for improving health outcomes, supporting long-term wellbeing, and in prevention.
This forms the basis of my commitment to sustainable, individualised strategies rather than
one-size-fits-all diets.
Each consultation involves a detailed understanding of the individual’s medical history,
symptoms, routine and lifestyle, cultural food preferences, and relationship with food. This
allows me to create realistic and tailored recommendations that can be integrated into daily
life.
I work across metabolic health, gut health, hormonal balance, and chronic disease
management - including conditions such as diabetes, insulin resistance, hypertension,
cardiovascular health, thyroid dysfunction, PCOS, fertility, perimenopause and menopause,
weight-related metabolic concerns and autoimmune conditions. I also provide specialist
support in cancer (oncology) nutrition and lifestyle, supporting individuals through
treatment and recovery within a multidisciplinary framework.
Alongside nutrition, I integrate key lifestyle factors such as movement, sleep, stress and
emotional wellbeing, and behaviour change to support lasting results. My focus is on
practical, culturally sensitive care, particularly when working with South Asian and
international populations, ensuring that recommendations are realistic, enjoyable,
sustainable, and tailored to each individual.
My goal is to help individuals feel informed, supported, and empowered to make lasting
changes that improve both their health and quality of life.
