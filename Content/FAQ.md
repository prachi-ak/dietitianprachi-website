What is the difference between a dietitian and a nutritionist?
Dietitians and nutritionists both work within the field of nutrition. However, Dietitian is a
protected professional title in the UK and can only be used by individuals registered with the
Health &amp; Care Professions Council (HCPC). This ensures that registered dietitians meet
recognised standards of education, training, and professional practice.

Do you offer online consultations?
Yes. Online consultations are available via secure video call for clients in the UK and
internationally (where appropriate). In-person consultations are also available at selected
locations in London.

How do I know which service is right for me?
If you are new to working with a dietitian or unsure where to start, the Free Discovery Call
is the best first step. This is a short, no-obligation conversation to discuss your goals and
help determine the most appropriate support for you.
If you are ready to begin nutrition and lifestyle support, the Initial Consultation is your first
full consultation, where we take a detailed look at your health, diet, lifestyle and goals and
create a personalised plan.
Follow-up consultations are for existing clients and are used to review progress, refine your
plan, and provide ongoing support.

What happens after I book my appointment?
Once your booking is received, Prachi will review it and confirm your appointment by email.
For paid consultations, payment is taken at the time of booking. You will then receive a
confirmation email with a calendar invitation and a secure video consultation link.

Do you work with specific medical conditions?
Yes. Prachi is a HCPC-registered dietitian with clinical experience in a range of healthcare
settings. Her approach is centred on providing individualised, evidence-based nutrition and
lifestyle support tailored to your medical history, lifestyle and personal goals. She works
with a wide range of conditions, including digestive health, weight management, hormonal
health, diabetes, oncology and general wellbeing. If you are unsure whether your condition
is suitable, the Free Discovery Call is the best way to discuss your needs and determine the
most appropriate support for you.

How should I prepare for my first appointment?
It can be helpful to keep a brief record of what you eat and drink for 2-3 days before your
consultation. If you have any recent blood test results or relevant medical information from
your GP, you are welcome to share these as well. Most importantly, come prepared with
your questions and an open mind so we can make the most of your session.

What is the cancellation policy?
Cancellations made more than 48 hours before your appointment are eligible for a full
refund. Cancellations made within 48 hours of the appointment are non-refundable.
However, you may reschedule your appointment with sufficient notice, subject to
availability. If you need to make any changes, please contact Prachi directly at the earliest.

Are your services covered by health insurance?
Some private health insurance providers in the UK may cover dietitian consultations,
depending on your policy. We recommend checking directly with your insurer before
booking to confirm your level of cover. Prachi is a HCPC-registered dietitian, which is
typically a requirement for insurance reimbursement.
